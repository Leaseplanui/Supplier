Lease Exchange

Exchange your out of warranty part for a Boeing leasable part. Typically you will pay the difference between the value of your part and the value of the part we provide you plus fees. You must obtain prior approval from Boeing for an exchange.

You may request an exchange of your unserviceable part for the Boeing leased part. Based partly upon information you provide, we will decide if an exchange is possible. The main criteria we use for accepting an exchanged part are interchangeability of the exchange part, if we want the part to be included in our lease pool, and the physical condition of your part.

To avoid lease charges, you must first receive a Preliminary Offer of Lease Exchange letter and then we must receive the exchanged part within 60 days of when you were shipped the leased part. Since this process takes time, notify First Responder as soon as possible (preferably within 10 days) after leasing a Boeing part. Do not send the part to Boeing until a Preliminary Offer of Lease Exchange letter is extended. Please keep in mind that Boeing must perform the evaluation and repair the damaged unit..

To Exchange a Part for a Boeing Leasable Part

The ordered quantity must be the same as the quoted quantity to ensure the quoted price. If the quantity is different, the price per unit may change as well. Any orders based on quotations must be placed prior to the expiration date to obtain the quoted price.

Order Type

Order types are as follows:

  • Sustaining: This is the default order type as most orders are processed as sustaining. The only exceptions are orders that need to be processed as one of the following order types:
  • Provisioning: This order type is used only to order parts that are included in a Recommended Spares Parts List (RSPL) or Peculiar Recommended Spare Parts List (PRSPL) to support new airplane deliveries with active initial provisioning programs.
  • Flight Simulator: This order type is used only to support Commercial Flight Simulator and Training Devices.
  • Lease: See Info Desk - Leasing Parts for order information.

Order Priorities

Ordering priorities are as follows:

  • AOG - Aircraft On Ground - aircraft registry designation, serial number or tail number required on the purchase order
  • Critical (CRT) - Imminent AOG or work stoppage
  • Expedite (EXP) - Parts required in less than normal lead time
  • Routine (RTN) - Parts required on a routine basis

To ensure that the correct priority is assigned, we request that you use the appropriate priority code in formatted orders, or the exact terminology, as stated above, if entering your priority orders in nonformatted messages. Boeing will not attempt to interpret any other terminology.

Service Bulletin Kits

To avoid delay in processing orders for service bulletin kits, please provide your modification / maintenance schedule including airplane serial numbers when placing your orders. To ensure fair distribution, when customer demand is greater than kit availability, we will base allocation of kits on customer schedules. If a schedule is not provided, your order may be suspended.

Color Coded Parts

Orders for color-coded spare parts must include the complete registry designation, serial number, or tail number of the applicable aircraft.

Interim Configuration for Spares

To facilitate production, Manufacturing is allowed to make interim configurations that vary from the Engineering definition of parts. Interim configurations are also required for some parts shipped as spares. These interim configurations are provided to the customer in a form necessary to facilitate installation.

See drawing 005W0900 for requirements and further information on the following interim configurations.

Undrilled Spare Parts

Spare parts designed by Boeing may be ordered without pilot holes by substituting a "U" in place of the "-" in the Boeing part number. The U-designated part number omits all undimensioned attaching fastener holes specified on any Boeing drawing. Undrilled spare parts do not omit lightening holes, cutouts, tooling holes, holes requiring bushings or bearings, or dimensioned attaching fastener holes.

For example:

Spare Part to Be Replaced Spare Part Identical to Production Part, Except Without Pilot Holes

141N5150-57 141N5150U57
65-12345-67 65-12345U67

"W" Part Numbers Spare Parts

The use of the letter "W" in place of the "-" in the dash number portion of a Boeing production part number identifies a spare or retrofit part identical to the production part except a component part such as a Chemical Oxygen Generator, Battery, etc., has been omitted for safety reasons.

"Y" Part Numbers Spare Parts

The use of the letter "Y" in place of the "-" in the dash number portion of a Boeing production part number identifies a spare or retrofit part identical to the original engineering part except for the following possible differences: additional fastener holes, undersized or omitted dimensioned holes, excess material or trim, additional final finish, and loose attachment of parts.
