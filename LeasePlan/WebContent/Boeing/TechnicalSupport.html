Technical Support

We provide technical information on technical spare part issues and orders, including AOG (Aircraft on the Ground) and critical orders 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This support is provided to all airline and repair station customers. Support is also provided to distributors with purchase orders. To obtain technical support, please contact First Responder.

Here is some of the information we provide:

Part Number Interchangeability -- We identify all authorized part substitutions.

Degree of Interchangeability

-1- One-way Interchangeable. Old part number may be used as replacement only where old part number was installed. New part number is an acceptable replacement for old or new part number.

-2- Two-way Interchangeable. Old or new part number may replace either old or new part number removed.

-5- Qualified Interchangeablility. Used when other interchangeable codes do not apply. Conditions of interchangeability must be explained in a separate, related note.

Part Identification -- We provide identification of unknown part numbers. Please provide a description of the part by location in the aircraft and/or documentation.

Part Number Usage -- We define where a part number is used on the airplane.

Part Number Effectivity -- We define the specific aircraft applicability.

Part Number Traceability -- We verify that the spare part is a Boeing product or used on a Boeing product.

Color Coded Part -- We determine color coded part numbers.

Document / Specification Clarification -- We provide Boeing technical document and specification clarification.

Production Part Numbers for AOG Usage -- We determine if production line specific part numbers can be used for AOG spares requirements.

Interim Configuration for Spares

To facilitate production, Manufacturing is allowed to make interim configurations that vary from the Engineering definition of parts. Interim configurations are also required for some parts shipped as spares. These interim configurations are provided to the customer in a form necessary to facilitate installation.

Undrilled Part Numbers -- We review for current manufacturing configuration for a "spare". This entails reviewing drawings for original configuration, (as only "piloted/fastener holes" can be omitted per the General Notes of the top drawing for each model, see 65-73701, GN13 for example), manufacturing plans and or supplier configuration. The "U" in place of a dash designates only the omission of all piloted or fastener hole locations.

See drawing 005W0900 for requirements and further information on the following interim configurations.

Undrilled Spare Parts

Spare parts designed by Boeing may be ordered without pilot holes by substituting a "U" in place of the "-" in the Boeing part number. The U-designated part number omits all undimensioned attaching fastener holes specified on any Boeing drawing. Undrilled spare parts do not omit lightening holes, cutouts, tooling holes, holes requiring bushings or bearings, or dimensioned attaching fastener holes.

For example:

Spare Part to Be Replaced Spare Part Identical to Production Part, Except Without Pilot Holes
141N5150-57 141N5150 U 57
65-12345-67 65-12345 U 67

"W" Part Numbers Spare Parts

The use of the letter "W" in place of the "-" in the dash number portion of a Boeing production part number identifies a spare or retrofit part identical to the production part except a component part such as a Chemical Oxygen Generator, Battery, etc., has been omitted for safety reasons.

"Y" Part Numbers Spare Parts

The use of the letter "Y" in place of the "-" in the dash number portion of a Boeing production part number identifies a spare or retrofit part identical to the original engineering part except for the following possible differences: additional fastener holes, undersized or omitted dimensioned holes, excess material or trim, additional final finish, and loose attachment of parts.

Raw Material Application / Substitution -- We identify raw material specifications for spare part usage, e.g. supplying a bulk of raw material which can be cut to length upon installation for an order for a proprietary part.

Component Breakdowns -- We determine what goes into an assembly or subassembly.

Shelf Life Issues -- We determine how long a product can be stored by identifying the specification of a "material" and identifying the shelf life and conditions of the product per the specification.
