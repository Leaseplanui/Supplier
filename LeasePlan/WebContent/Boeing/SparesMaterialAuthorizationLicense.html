Spares Material Authorization License (SMAL) Request

A Spares Material Authorization License (SMAL) can be requested when the following requirements have been met:

  • At least one aircraft operated or maintained by the customer is unable to be dispatched within 10 days, solely due to the unavailability of the part.
  • The part is a Boeing or Douglas model proprietary detail/component part built per engineering drawing only and is not an assembly, installation, kit, supplier proprietary part, undrilled part, noted condition part, repair, wire bundle, raw material, or a part made using alternate materials and or processes not already called out in the engineering drawing.
  • A current insurance certificate is on file with Boeing.
  • Customer has previously executed a Boeing CSGTA, HMSGTA, or MRO-A agreement.
  • Customer agrees to not use any Boeing proprietary materials and information provided or licensed in connection with granting the SMAL request for any purpose other than the manufacture of a single ship set needed to return the identified AOG aircraft back to service.
  • Customer has part fabrication services available to manufacture a new part by the specified return to service date.
  • An AOG priority purchase order for at least one ship set of a part has been submitted to Boeing.
  • Customer has called / contacted First Responder and provided the following Tier I information along with their intent to request a SMAL. Failure to call First Responder prior to submitting your SMAL request may result in your SMAL being denied.

Tier I Information

  • Aircraft serial number
  • Detailed aircraft situation
  • Date part(s) needed on dock
  • Aircraft scheduled return to service date
  • 24 hour contact information

Owner/Operators with a signed CSGTA are not required to request a SMAL on simple parts made with simple tools valued up to 100.00 USD.

Spares Material Authorization License (SMAL) Confirmation Page

Your SMAL Form has been sent to the AOG Desk. We will review your request and will advise you of the final disposition upon completion of our review.

If the aircraft is due to return to service within 72 hours:

  • Boeing will attempt to provide the customer with a part solution and/or a proposed ship date within 24 hours of submitting a SMAL request.
  • If no parts solution can be found, or if the customer cannot accept the proposed ship date, the SMAL may be granted.

All other requests with a return to service date within 10 days will be worked in accordance to the AOG process

  • Boeing will attempt to provide a part solution and/or a proposed ship date to the customer that will meet the aircraft return to service date.
  • If no parts solution can be found, the SMAL may be granted.

Upon SMAL approval, drawings obtained through MyBoeingFleet.com will be available for seven days. Drawings in physical formats will be subject to the current Data and Services Management catalog prices.

SMAL cannot be processed in cases where the associated customer order is on a credit hold.

IF the SMAL is granted, the related Customer purchase order will remain open until Boeing receives a request from the customer to cancel such order. Cancellation charges may apply.
